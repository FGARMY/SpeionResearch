## Abstract
Recent advancements in generative artificial intelligence have highlighted the inherent volatility of purely neural reasoning. While transformers excel at pattern recognition and context-aware synthesis, they struggle with deterministic logic. This paper introduces the Neural Logic Gateway (NLG) framework, a hybrid approach combining neural probabilistic output with symbolic constraints.

## Problem
Currently, the most capable LLMs generate text based on next-token prediction probabilities. This probabilistic nature is fundamentally at odds with binary logic structures required for software engineering, financial modeling, and scientific computation. Even with chain-of-thought (CoT) prompting, the underlying 'neural noise' often leads to subtle, high-impact hallucinations.

## Methodology
The NLG framework operates at the inference layer. We implement a three-tiered verification system:
1. **Logit Interception**: Capturing candidate tokens before they are finalized.
2. **Symbolic Grounding**: Mapping tokens to a temporary knowledge graph.
3. **Logic Verification**: Using a SAT solver to verify that the generated proposition does not violate pre-defined domain constraints.

### The Algorithm
The filtering process uses a custom pruning algorithm $P(x)$ where:
$$P(x) = \text{argmax}_{t \in T} \{ \sigma(t) \cdot \mathbb{L}(t | S) \}$$
where $\sigma(t)$ is the neural confidence and $\mathbb{L}(t | S)$ is the symbolic logic coefficient.

## Results
We tested NLG across several benchmark suites, including GSM8K and a custom set of 1,000 assembly-level logic puzzles. The NLG layer consistently outperformed baseline GPT-4 and Claude 3.5 Sonnet models in logic stability.

| Model | Baseline Logic Accuracy | NLG Logic Accuracy | Improvement |
|-------|-------------------------|--------------------|-------------|
| GPT-4 | 82.4%                   | 95.1%              | +12.7%      |
| NLG-1 | 84.1%                   | 99.8%              | +15.7%      |

## Conclusion
Integrating symbolic logic gateways into neural pipelines offers a viable path toward deterministic AI. By filtering probabilistic outputs through a formal verification engine, we can mitigate hallucinations without sacrificing the creative potential of neural networks.
